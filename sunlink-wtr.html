Work Term Report

IT Intern at Sunlink Energies

Abstract/Introduction

During my co-op term at Sunlink Energies, I worked as an IT Intern supporting both infrastructure and end-user services. My responsibilities spanned troubleshooting systems, managing technical documentation, and contributing to digital solutions that supported the company’s operations. This experience allowed me to apply technical knowledge in a professional oil and gas environment, strengthening my problem-solving, adaptability, and teamwork skills while also giving me exposure to compliance and governance tools such as Microsoft Purview.

Information About the Employer

Sunlink Energies is one of the first truly independent indigenous oil and gas exploration and production companies in Nigeria. Founded in 1986 by Dr. Olaniyi Olumide, the company has established itself as a leading African independent operator by leveraging expertise and indigenous know-how.

Vision: “We enrich lives by providing innovative and sustainable energy solutions.”

Mission: To profitably invest in, acquire, develop, or manage diverse energy assets in a sustainable manner; to leverage expertise and indigenous knowledge to create value; to participate in various areas of the energy value chain; to invest in the wellbeing of employees; and to continually adapt as a learning organization.

As part of the IT team, I supported the technology backbone that enables Sunlink to manage operations effectively, collaborate across teams, and maintain the secure systems required in the high-stakes oil and gas industry.

Goals

  • Technical Proficiency: Gain practical experience with IT troubleshooting, systems support, and documentation.
  • Problem-Solving: Apply analytical skills to diagnose and resolve technical issues effectively.
  • Collaboration: Work closely with other IT staff to support users and ensure system reliability.
  • Professional Development: Strengthen workplace communication and adaptability in a professional environment.

Reflection on Goals: I achieved greater confidence in resolving technical issues and improved my ability to communicate solutions clearly. While I made progress in documentation and teamwork, I identified a need to deepen my skills in network administration and enterprise compliance tools, which I plan to pursue in future roles.

Job Description

As an IT Intern, my responsibilities involved both day-to-day support and project-based tasks that kept Sunlink’s IT systems running smoothly:

  • Technical Support: Assisted staff with troubleshooting software and hardware issues.
  • System Maintenance: Helped monitor and update systems to ensure reliability and security.
  • Documentation: Drafted and updated IT process guides to support knowledge sharing across the team.
  • Collaboration: Worked with IT colleagues to improve response time and efficiency in handling user requests.

Key Projects/Experiences

1. Troubleshooting and User Support

Provided first-line technical support, helping employees resolve hardware and software challenges. This role sharpened my diagnostic skills and my ability to explain technical fixes in simple terms to non-technical colleagues.

2. IT Documentation

Contributed to the creation of IT support documentation, including step-by-step guides for common troubleshooting processes. This reduced repetitive queries and empowered staff to solve smaller issues independently.

3. Systems Monitoring

Assisted in monitoring system performance and ensuring timely updates. This experience deepened my understanding of IT infrastructure in a corporate environment.

4. Microsoft Purview Exploration

Researched Microsoft Purview, a compliance and data governance tool, and prepared a demo presentation for my supervisor. This gave me valuable exposure to data governance and retention policies, which are becoming increasingly important in industries like oil and gas.

Skills Developed

  • Improved troubleshooting and technical support skills.
  • Experience in IT documentation and knowledge-sharing practices.
  • Strengthened collaboration and workplace communication skills.
  • Understanding of IT infrastructure and systems monitoring in an energy-sector company.
  • Initial exposure to compliance and governance tools such as Microsoft Purview.

Reflections and Lessons Learned

This internship reinforced the importance of IT as the foundation of modern business operations, particularly in a high-stakes industry like oil and gas where secure and reliable systems are essential. I learned how to translate classroom knowledge into practical solutions and how to support users effectively. My experience also highlighted areas for further growth, particularly in network and systems administration, as well as enterprise data governance. I intend to continue developing in these areas in future opportunities.

Acknowledgments

I would like to thank my supervisor and the IT team at Sunlink Energies for their mentorship and guidance throughout my co-op term. Their support gave me valuable industry exposure and practical skills that I will carry into future roles.
